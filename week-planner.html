Week Planner #

Description #

This is a tool that's part of a larger project (Academic Control System). Its purpose is to provide an easy way to manage the schedules of groups, teachers, students and anything that relies on a schedule.

This was designed specifically for the university that the main project belongs.

Core features #

Overlaps avoidance #

To avoid overlaps, the tool can merge two or more schedules in the timetable so it's easy to spot free spaces between them.

For example, in the video above we have the schedule of the 4ºA group open and its classes are on the left side panel, when the user clicks on a class the tool loads the teacher's schedule of that class and merges it with the schedule of the group. Every blue item belongs to the owner (4ºA group) of the tab and has the priority of being on front of everything when overlapping.

Every item that doesn't belong to the owner of the tab has the name of its owner on the bottom left side and is red or green:

Red means you can't overlap while green means you can.

Overlaps detection #

When an overlap is detected on a cell, a blue notification is displayed with the amount of overlapping items. A popup with the overlapping items will appear if the overlap notification is clicked (as you can see in the image above).

Tabs #

Tabs are a very important part of the tool, they provide an easy way to work with multiple schedules at the same time without the need to leave or refresh the page. Each tab works on its own and they are reactive, so changes are reflected on every tab when necessary.

Hide items on the timetable #

When schedules are merged the timetable can be bloated and depending on the situation, the user may want to see only certain types of activities so the tool allows to hide them.

Pin elements on the timetable #

It is possible to pin elements on the timetable therefore allowing to merge an infinite number of schedules on it. In the video above we merge the schedules of teachers Theodore, Marco, Samira and the 4ºA group.
